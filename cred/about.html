Why we exist.

Provider groups should focus on patients, not paperwork. Every week a provider spends un-billable is revenue you never recover. We exist to get your clinicians helping patients faster.

01 Credentialing is treated as admin

It sits with whoever has capacity, gets picked up between other duties, and nobody owns the date the file actually clears.

02 Nobody models the cost

Practices can quote a provider's salary to the dollar but not the revenue lost while that provider waits on a payer.

03 Rework restarts the clock

An incomplete file or a stale attestation does not just delay a submission — it sends you to the back of the queue.

04 Renewals arrive unannounced

Licenses, DEA registrations, and re-credentialing cycles lapse quietly, and the first signal is a denied claim.

How we work.

The same operating approach on every engagement, whether it is one provider or a network.

Audit

We inventory every provider, license, and payer relationship, and find what is expired, incomplete, or stalled.

Prioritize

We sequence the roster by revenue at risk, so the work that unblocks billing happens first.

File

Complete packets the first time — verified, attested, and built to survive payer review without rework.

Monitor

Standing follow-up on every submission, and deadline tracking so renewals never surprise you.

Who is behind Gusty Group.

Gusty Group is led by founder Chris Gusty. Chris spent the last decade building in healthcare — most recently as COO at Mend Labs, where he scaled a venture-backed healthcare company from founding to 40+ employees, led $25M+ in fundraising, and built the operating model, finance function, revenue cycle, and licensing, credentialing, and enrollment functions from scratch. Before that he advised large organizations on technology and operations strategy at Deloitte.

Chris is a Certified Revenue Cycle Representative (HFMA) and a Financial Modeling & Valuation Analyst (CFI).
