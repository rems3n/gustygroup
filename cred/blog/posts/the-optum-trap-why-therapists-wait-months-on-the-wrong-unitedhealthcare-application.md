Quick answer: If you're a therapist, counselor, psychologist, or LCSW, you do not credential with UnitedHealthcare directly; you credential with Optum Behavioral Health (via Provider Express). Applications filed on the medical side sit in the wrong queue indefinitely. The same split exists at Cigna, where behavioral health routes through Evernorth.

## A real scenario (that happens constantly)

An LMFT opens a private practice, googles "join UnitedHealthcare network," lands on UHC's provider site, and submits an application. Sixty days pass. She calls. The rep can't find her file. Another thirty days. Eventually someone explains: behavioral health providers are credentialed by Optum, a separate entity with a separate portal, a separate application, and a separate panel. Her three months of waiting counted for nothing.

This isn't a rare mistake. It's the single most common credentialing error we see among therapists, because nothing on the front end of the process tells you which track you're on.

## How the routing actually works

Big payers split their networks in two:

- Medical network - physicians, NPs, dietitians, PTs, chiropractors, and most non-behavioral clinicians.
- Behavioral health network - outsourced to a managed behavioral health organization (MBHO) that runs its own credentialing, contracting, and fee schedules.

The major ones:

- UnitedHealthcare → Optum Behavioral Health (Provider Express portal)
- Cigna → Evernorth Behavioral Health
- Aetna → Aetna Behavioral Health (internal, but separate application track)
- Many BCBS plans → Varies by state — some internal, some carved out (e.g., to Magellan or Carelon)

## Which track are you on? Check your license type

- Behavioral track: LPC, LMFT, LCSW, LMHC, licensed psychologists, psychiatric NPs (varies by payer), BCBAs.
- Medical track: RDs, PTs, OTs, SLPs, physicians, most NPs.
- It depends: Psychiatric NPs and psychiatrists sometimes credential on both sides. Ask before filing.

Rule of thumb: if you bill 908xx CPT codes (psychotherapy), you belong on the behavioral track.

## Already filed on the wrong side? Do this

- Don't wait for a rejection: wrong-track applications often just sit; they aren't always denied.
- Call the behavioral entity directly (e.g., Optum Provider Express support), confirm nothing exists under your NPI, and start the correct application immediately.
- Withdraw the medical-side application in writing so a stray file doesn't create duplicate-record problems later.
- Ensure your CAQH profile and NPI taxonomy reflect a behavioral health taxonomy code. A medical taxonomy on a behavioral application is its own rejection trigger.

## Check panel status before you apply

One more trap: behavioral health panels close. Optum in particular maintains closed panels in saturated metro areas. Before spending 90 days on an application, call and ask whether the panel is open for your license type and county. If it's closed, ask about the appeal/exception process (demonstrated need, niche specialty, language capacity).

## FAQ

**How long does Optum credentialing take?**

Typically 60-120 days from a complete Provider Express application, plus contracting time. Closed-panel exceptions take longer.

**Is Provider Express the same as UnitedHealthcare's provider portal?**

No. Provider Express is Optum Behavioral Health's system. UHC's medical portal will not show or advance a behavioral application.

**Do I need separate CAQH profiles for Optum and UHC?**

No. One CAQH profile serves both, but your taxonomy, specialties, and practice data must be accurate and re-attested.
